Math Gang Collatz Experiment

Edit the inputs in a panel, run that calculation, and review the JSON output below. Large samples are capped by the local Flask API to keep runs responsive.

Static

Distribution and Residue Summaries

Use these for aggregate snapshots over sampled integers: frequencies, mean drift, autocorrelation, and residue-class differences.

v Distribution and Drift

Samples odd integers from start, then estimates v frequencies, log drift, and autocorrelation.

Residue-Class Drift

Groups sampled odd integers by n mod m and reports drift plus high-v event frequency.

Dynamic

Transition Maps

Use these to study how states move: residue classes under the odd-only map and empirical transitions between consecutive v values.

Residue Transitions

Tracks how odd residue classes move under T(n) = (3n + 1) / 2^v.

v Transition Matrix

Estimates empirical transition probabilities P(v next | v current) from sampled trajectories.

Temporal

Paths and Waiting Times

Use these for step-indexed behavior: complete trajectories and first-hit timing for strong contraction events.

Trajectory

Computes the full Collatz path, odd-only path, log path, deltas, v sequence, and summary.

Time to v Threshold

Measures odd-only steps until a trajectory first reaches v greater than or equal to the threshold.

Cumulative Drift

Tracks S_k = sum(log(3) - v_k log(2)) along odd-only steps for sampled trajectories.

Expansion Run Lengths

Measures consecutive odd-only steps where v = 1, the pure expansion case.
